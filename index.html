About Us

Learn about our journey and what drives us forward

Who We Are

We are a research team at the University of Edinburgh specializing in applied superconductivity, electric motor design, and cryogenic cooling systems.

Our group consists of professors, lecturers, postdoctoral researchers, and PhD students working together to overcome practical challenges in implementing superconducting technologies in electrical machines.

Current research focuses on developing high-efficiency superconducting motors, optimizing cryogenic systems, and exploring applications in renewable energy, transportation, and industrial sectors.

Our Vision

To advance the practical implementation of superconducting technologies in electrical machines.

We envision a future where superconducting motors contribute to more efficient and sustainable energy systems across transportation, industrial, and renewable energy applications.

Our Mission

To develop viable superconducting motor designs and cryogenic cooling solutions that overcome current technological barriers.

We accomplish this by combining expertise in electrical engineering, materials science, and thermal management, while maintaining a focus on both scientific advancement and practical application.

Our Projects

Explore some of our current work

Superconduncting Wind Turbine

Project One

A Royal Academy of Engineering funded project developing superconducting wind turbine generators with increased power density. Our research aims to overcome size and weight constraints in conventional designs while improving energy conversion efficiency.

Learn More
Aerospace Motor

Project Two

Development of high-speed superconducting motors for aerospace applications. This research addresses the challenges of creating lightweight, compact propulsion systems with superior power-to-weight ratios to enable more efficient and sustainable aircraft designs.

Learn More
Flux Pump

Project Three

Contactless flux pump design enables contactless excitation of large-scale superconducting wind turbine rotor coils, charges high-inductance coils, significantly reduces system losses, and provides assurance for the excitation of large superconducting wind turbines.

Learn More

Our Laboratory

Discover our research facilities and equipment

The Superconducting Machine Laboratory at the University of Edinburgh is equipped with state-of-the-art facilities for developing and testing superconducting electrical machines and components.

Our specialized equipment includes:

  • Cryogenic testing chambers for evaluating superconducting components at temperatures down to 20K
  • High-precision electromagnetic measurement systems
  • Advanced manufacturing facilities for prototype development
  • Computational resources for electromagnetic and thermal simulation
  • Characterization equipment for superconducting materials

The laboratory provides a collaborative environment where researchers, students, and industry partners can work together to advance superconducting machine technology from fundamental research to practical applications.
