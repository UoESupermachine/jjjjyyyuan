Transforming Wind Energy

Royal Academy of Engineering Flagship Project

Engineering Excellence Beyond Limits

Our groundbreaking research at the University of Edinburgh represents a paradigm shift in wind turbine technology. Supported by the Royal Academy of Engineering, we're developing superconducting generators that redefine what's possible in renewable energy.

Mission-Critical Objectives

  • Achieve revolutionary 40% weight reduction through advanced superconducting materials
  • Engineer cryogenic systems capable of withstanding extreme offshore conditions
  • Optimize electromagnetic designs for maximum flux density and minimal losses
  • Demonstrate economic viability for next-generation 15-20MW turbines
  • Accelerate global transition to sustainable energy infrastructure

This transformative technology positions the UK at the forefront of the global renewable energy revolution, enabling offshore wind farms to generate clean power at unprecedented scales and efficiencies.

Breakthrough Technology

High-Temperature Superconductors

Our advanced HTS materials operate at temperatures achievable with modern cryogenic systems, eliminating the need for expensive liquid helium cooling. This breakthrough enables practical deployment in offshore environments.

  • REBCO and MgB‚ÇÇ superconducting tapes with critical current densities exceeding 1000 A/mm¬≤
  • Operation at 20-40K using efficient closed-cycle cryocoolers
  • Zero electrical resistance for unprecedented efficiency
  • Robust performance under high magnetic fields up to 5T
Advanced Design

Axial Flux Topology

Our innovative axial flux design with superconducting rotor coils delivers exceptional power density while maintaining structural simplicity. This configuration is optimized for large-scale offshore turbines.

  • Compact form factor with 35% reduction in nacelle length
  • Direct-drive configuration eliminating gearbox failures
  • Modular construction for enhanced maintainability
  • Optimized magnetic circuit minimizing flux leakage
Thermal Management

Next-Gen Cryogenic Systems

Our proprietary cooling technology maintains optimal superconductor performance while minimizing parasitic power consumption. The system is designed for 25+ years of maintenance-free operation.

  • Redundant cooling paths ensuring 99.9% uptime
  • Advanced thermal insulation reducing heat ingress by 80%
  • Intelligent control systems optimizing cooling efficiency
  • Salt-spray resistant design for marine environments

Research Impact & Milestones

Our journey towards revolutionizing wind energy

2021

Project Inception

Secured ¬£5M Royal Academy of Engineering funding. Assembled world-class research team. Initiated fundamental superconductor characterization studies.

2022

Design Breakthrough

Completed revolutionary axial flux topology design. Achieved 40% weight reduction in simulations. Filed 3 key patents on cooling system innovations.

Prototype Success
