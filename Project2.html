Project Overview

Advancing Electric Aircraft Propulsion

Our aerospace motor research program focuses on developing compact, lightweight superconducting motors specifically designed for aircraft propulsion systems. We're addressing the critical challenges of creating propulsion solutions with superior power-to-weight ratios that can enable more efficient and sustainable aircraft designs.

Key project objectives include:

  • Design and develop superconducting motors with power densities exceeding 15 kW/kg, more than double current state-of-the-art
  • Create lightweight cryogenic cooling systems suitable for aviation applications
  • Optimize rotor and stator designs for high-efficiency operation across flight regimes
  • Develop fault-tolerant architectures for aviation-grade reliability
  • Demonstrate technology readiness through comprehensive ground testing program

This research is conducted in collaboration with leading aerospace manufacturers and has applications in hybrid-electric regional aircraft, urban air mobility, and future all-electric commercial aviation.

Aerospace Innovation Highlights

✈️ Unmatched Power Density

Our aerospace motors achieve breakthrough power densities of 15+ kW/kg—more than double current state-of-the-art technology. This exceptional performance is achieved through a combination of advanced superconducting materials, optimized electromagnetic design, and innovative cooling integration.

This power density breakthrough is a key enabler for electric aircraft, allowing propulsion systems that maintain sufficient power without the prohibitive weight penalties that have previously limited electric aviation.

🌡️

Flight-Compatible Cooling

Our lightweight cryogenic systems integrate directly with aircraft thermal management architecture while meeting stringent aviation requirements for weight, reliability, and safety. The system maintains optimal superconductor temperature with minimal power consumption during all flight phases.

80% lighter than conventional cryogenic systems
⚙️

Aviation-Grade Reliability

Our motors feature triple-redundant architecture with graceful degradation capabilities, ensuring continuous operation even if component failures occur. Every system undergoes rigorous testing to meet DO-160 environmental standards and DO-178C software certification requirements.

99.997% reliability demonstrated in testing

Project Roadmap

2023-2024
Concept Development & Design

Completion of initial design, electromagnetic simulations, and material selection. Development of detailed cooling system concept.

2024-2025
Component Testing

Fabrication and testing of critical components including HTS coils, cooling system, and control electronics. Validation of key technologies.

2025-2026
Prototype Development

Assembly and ground testing of 250kW prototype motor. Performance verification and design optimization.

2026-2027
Full-Scale Demonstration

Construction and testing of full-scale 1MW motor system under simulated flight conditions. Validation of performance metrics.

2027-2028
Technology Transfer

Collaboration with industry partners for technology maturation and preparation for integration into demonstration aircraft.
