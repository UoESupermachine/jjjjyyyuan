Dr Hongye Zhang

Board Member of the European Society for Applied Superconductivity (ESAS)
Board Member of the HTS Modelling Workgroup
Early Career Editorial Board member & Associate Editor for the Elsevier journal Superconductivity
University of Edinburgh

Dr. Hongye Zhang is a Lecturer (Assistant Professor) in Superconducting/Cryogenic Electric Machines at the University of Edinburgh and a Visiting Research Fellow at the University of Manchester. He holds a PhD in Applied Superconductivity from The University of Edinburgh (2021), as well as an MSc and BSc in Electrical Engineering from Xi'an Jiaotong University, China, and a Diplôme d’ingénieur from École Centrale de Lyon, France.

In 2021, Dr. Zhang received the prestigious IEEE Council on Superconductivity Graduate Study Fellowship and has been involved in the £54-million H2GEAR project at The University of Manchester since then. His research focuses on the design, analysis, and testing of superconducting and cryogenic electric machines, as well as the application of hydrogen energy and artificial intelligence in clean energy systems.

Dr. Zhang is actively engaged in the academic community, serving as a Board Member of the European Society for Applied Superconductivity (ESAS) and the HTS Modelling Workgroup, and as an Early Career Editorial Board member & Associate Editor for the *Elsevier Journal of Superconductivity*. He is also a Technical Editor for IEEE Transactions on Applied Superconductivity and a Programme Committee Member for SMT 2023.

His interdisciplinary research integrates Electric Machines & Drives, Superconductor Technology, Cryogenic Techniques, Hydrogen Energy, and AI, with a particular focus on power-dense electric propulsion systems for Net Zero Transport. His long-term goal is to establish a world-leading research group that bridges academia and industry to drive the decarbonization of future transport systems.
