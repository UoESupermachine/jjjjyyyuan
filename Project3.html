Project Overview

Revolutionizing Superconducting Coil Excitation

Our flux pump research focuses on developing contactless excitation technologies for large-scale superconducting coils. This innovation eliminates the need for conventional current leads, significantly reducing thermal losses and improving system efficiency.

Key project objectives include:

  • Design and optimize flux pump topologies capable of exciting high-inductance superconducting coils
  • Develop reliable operation methodologies for long-term persistent current operation
  • Create compact and efficient designs suitable for integration with wind turbine generators
  • Minimize thermal losses and increase overall system efficiency
  • Demonstrate technology at increasing scale and under relevant operating conditions

This technology is particularly critical for large superconducting wind turbine generators, where conventional excitation methods would introduce prohibitive thermal losses and mechanical challenges.

How Flux Pump Technology Works

1

Magnetic Field Variation

The flux pump creates controlled variations in magnetic field that interact with a superconducting circuit. This is typically achieved through rotating permanent magnets or oscillating magnetic fields.

2

Flux Quantization

These field variations induce quantized flux motion within the superconductor, creating a net DC voltage across a superconducting bridge element.

3

Current Accumulation

This DC voltage drives current into the superconducting load coil, with current building up gradually over multiple operational cycles.

4

Persistent Current Mode

Once the desired current level is reached, the flux pump can be operated in maintenance mode or switched off, with the current persisting in the superconducting circuit with minimal decay.

    Operational Parameters

    Our flux pump system operates with the following key parameters:

    • Operational temperature range: 20-40K
    • Maximum excitation voltage: 100-500 μV
    • Charging rate: 0.1-1 A/min depending on load inductance
    • Maximum achievable current: 200A in 10H coil demonstrated
    • Long-term stability: current decay rate below 0.1% per day
    • Power consumption: less than 20W for pump operation
    • Operational reliability: MTBF >10,000 hours

    These parameters have been validated through extensive laboratory testing and demonstrate substantial improvements over conventional excitation methods.

    Integration Solutions

    Our flux pump technology has been designed for seamless integration with various superconducting systems:

    • Modular attachment system for wind turbine generator field coils
    • Cryostat-compatible mechanical interfaces
    • Thermal isolation structures to minimize heat transfer
    • Standardized electrical connections for monitoring and control
    • Vibration-resistant mounting for rotating applications
    • Compact form factor for space-constrained installations

    Integration solutions are developed in close collaboration with system designers to ensure optimal performance in the target application.

    Performance Metrics

    Our technology has achieved the following performance metrics:

    • Thermal efficiency: >90% reduction in heat load compared to conventional current leads
    • Excitation stability: field variation < 0.1% over 24 hours
    • System reliability: successful operation through >100 thermal cycles
    • Scale testing: demonstrated on coils up to 10H inductance
    • Mechanical stability: operational under 3G acceleration conditions
    • Control precision: current regulation to within ±0.5% of target value

    These metrics have been verified through rigorous testing programs and represent state-of-the-art performance for contactless excitation systems.

    Research Results

    High-Inductance Coil Excitation

    We successfully demonstrated excitation of a 10H superconducting coil to 200A using our contactless flux pump technology. The excitation process exhibited stable operation with minimal thermal impact on the cryogenic system.

    The results confirm the viability of this approach for large-scale wind turbine applications where high field currents must be maintained with minimal power input.

    Long-term Stability Testing

    Extended operation testing over 1000 hours demonstrated exceptional current stability with decay rates below 0.1% per day. The system maintained reliable operation through multiple simulated operational scenarios.

    These results confirm the technology's readiness for integration into critical applications where long-term field stability is essential.
