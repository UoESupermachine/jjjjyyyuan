Professor Markus Mueller

Royal Academy of Engineering Chair in Emerging Technologies
Professor of Electrical Machines, School of Engineering
University of Edinburgh

Since 2012, Professor Markus Mueller has held a Personal Chair in Electrical Generation Systems at the University of Edinburgh, having been appointed as a lecturer in 2004. In 2023 he was awarded a Royal Academy of Engineering Chair in Emerging Technologies to investigate new high power density high temperature superconducting machines for net-zero energy and transport applications.

From 2014 to 2018, he led a group of 26 academics as Head of the Institute for Energy Systems, and is now co-leading Energy@Edinburgh, a cross university community of 200+ academics and researchers focussing on energy systems. He has supported 25 PhD students to successful graduation as principal supervisor, and supervised 18 PDRAs.

His research focuses on electrical generators for renewable energy converters, and the development of hybrid energy systems integrating renewables and energy storage for on and off-grid use. Since 1997 he has been awarded £13m in grant funding, 75% as PI, from various sources including Royal Academy of Engineering, EPSRC, Innovate UK, The Carbon Trust, Scottish Enterprise, Wave Energy Scotland, Scottish Government, EU FP6, FP7, H2020 and ERDF, as well as direct industrial funding.

Professor Mueller has 258 journal and conference publications, with an h-index of 38 and 6294 citations (2887 since 2017). He co-edited the book "Electrical Drives for Direct Drive Renewable Energy Systems" and has been awarded 3 patents. In 2009 he spun out NGenTec Ltd to commercialise the C-GEN technology for offshore wind, which raised £7m from private and public sources.
